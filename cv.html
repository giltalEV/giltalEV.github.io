Research Program

Research on the transition to electric transportation, focusing on consumer adoption, charging behavior and infrastructure, and the global electric vehicle supply chain. Work informs policy at the state, federal, and international levels through close partnerships with the California Air Resources Board, the California Energy Commission, the U.S. Department of Transportation, and counterparts in Mexico, Europe, and the Global South.

Primary Research Areas: EV market and adoption (behavioral research on first-time buyers, repeat adopters, used-vehicle markets, and discontinuance); charging behavior and infrastructure (commute charging, DC fast charging reliability, real-world driver data); global EV supply chains (MONET model — first framework integrating global supply and market data); equity and the energy transition (disadvantaged communities, secondary ZEV market in California).
