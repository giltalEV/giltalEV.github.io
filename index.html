I lead research on the transition to electric transportation, focusing on consumer adoption, charging behavior and infrastructure, and the global electric vehicle supply chain. My work informs policy at the state, federal, and international levels through close partnerships with the California Air Resources Board, the California Energy Commission, the U.S. Department of Transportation, and counterparts in Mexico, Europe, and the Global South.

Research

My research program at the UC Davis Electric Vehicle Research Center is built around sustained, multi-year data collection efforts that address barriers to transportation electrification at state, national, and international scales. The work is multidisciplinary and combines survey experiments, longitudinal panel data, behavioral modeling, and global trade analysis. It is organized around three primary research areas.

EV market and adoption

Behavioral research on first-time buyers, repeat adopters, used-vehicle markets, and discontinuance, drawing on the Diffusion of Innovation framework and latent-class modeling of California vehicle purchasers.

Charging behavior and infrastructure

Pioneering work adapting travel behavior methods to EV charging, including commute charging, DC fast charging reliability, and large-scale data collection on real-world driver charging experiences.

Global EV supply chains

Development of MONET (Model for International Electric Vehicle Trade), the first framework integrating global supply and market data to estimate worldwide EV production. Now applied by the Mexican federal government for industrial and environmental policy.

Equity and the energy transition

Analysis of how aggressive electrification targets distribute costs across population segments, with attention to disadvantaged communities and the secondary ZEV market in California.
